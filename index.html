Tenacity is an easy-to-use, cross-platform multi-track audio editor/recorder for Windows, MacOS, GNU/Linux and other operating systems and is developed by a group of volunteers as open-source software.

Features

Recording
from audio devices (real or virtual)

Export & Import
a wide range of audio formats (extendible with FFmpeg)

High Quality
including up to 32-bit float audio support

Plug-Ins
providing support for VST, LV2, and AU plugins

Scripting
in the built-in scripting language Nyquist, or in Python, Perl and other languages with named pipes

Editing
arbitrary sampling and multi-track timeline

Accessibility
including editing via keyboard, screen reader support and narration support

Tools
useful in the analysis of signals, including audio

Getting Started

Prepackaged builds will be available through the releases page on the repository once the initial process of transitioning the project is completed enough for the first release version. Packaging Tenacity to other package managers is already planned

Alternatively, you can build Tenacity from source. See BUILDING.md

Getting Help

A great place to ask for general questions with using Tenacity, starting discussions about your ideas and feature requests, and generally getting to know the community is the tenacity-discuss mailing list .

You may also use our IRC channel, #tenacity (hosted on irc.libera.chat),
where you're welcome to ask questions and have discussions in a more free-form, real-time context. The channel is also bridged to Matrix.

Development

The upstream development repository may be found on sr.ht.

Contribute by sending patches to the
development mailing list at ~tenacity/tenacity-dev@lists.sr.ht.
